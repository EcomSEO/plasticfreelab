# PlasticFreeLab — Brand Book

The voice, audience, and visual identity that every post, pin, and page on plasticfreelab.com must reflect.

---

## 1. Who we are

PlasticFreeLab is the most trustworthy non-toxic living resource on the internet. We test, we cite, we update, and we tell readers what's actually worth replacing — not what's currently trending on Instagram.

We are not a wellness blog. We are not a mommy site. We are not a fearmongering "everything is poison" panic outlet. We are the calm, evidence-led friend who already did the research and will tell you the three things in your kitchen that actually matter and the seven things you can stop worrying about.

## 2. The reader

### Primary persona — "Sarah, 34"

She's married, has one child (2-year-old), works part-time as a marketing manager. Household income $95k-$140k. Lives in a mid-sized US city — Austin, Denver, Charlotte, Portland, somewhere with a Whole Foods. She has a Costco membership and an Amazon Prime account.

She started caring about non-toxic living when she got pregnant. Read *Real Food for Pregnancy*, joined a few Facebook groups, switched to fragrance-free everything for a few months. Then her kid was born and she got overwhelmed by the volume of conflicting information and fell off most of it. Now her kid is 2 and she wants to *do this properly* without losing her mind or her budget.

She's smart. She's not gullible. She rolled her eyes the last time someone told her about "frequencies" or "vibrations." She wants peer-reviewed sources, real test results, and a straight answer.

She's frustrated by:
- "Crunchy mom" content that feels performatively scared
- 4,000-word blog posts that take 30 minutes to get to the actual recommendation
- Affiliate sites that obviously haven't tested what they're recommending
- Conflicting advice on fluoride, sunscreen, seed oils
- Her own mother dismissing all of this as "you're being paranoid"

She trusts:
- EWG (cautiously — she knows they overreact sometimes)
- Peer-reviewed studies, when they're explained well
- Specific people: Vani Hari sometimes, Lily Nichols, Aubrey Marcus, Kelly Brogan (with reservations)
- Wirecutter (loves it for non-toxic categories but wishes they had more)

### Secondary personas

- **"Marcus, 41"** — male, fitness-focused, listens to Huberman, optimizing his household after reading about microplastics in testes
- **"Amanda, 52"** — empty-nester, post-menopausal, dealing with a thyroid issue, suspecting environmental factors
- **"Jenna, 28"** — pre-pregnancy, planning ahead, aggressive non-toxic switcher

We write for Sarah by default. The other personas are tertiary — they come along for the ride.

## 3. The promise

> We test the swaps so you don't have to.

Three things every PlasticFreeLab post must deliver:

1. **A specific, ranked recommendation** — never "here are some options to consider," always "this is the best one and here's why"
2. **The science, in plain English** — never "studies show," always "[specific study, year, n=sample size, finding]"
3. **A budget tier** — best overall, best mid-range, best on a tight budget, what to buy first if you can only afford one

## 4. Voice

### How we sound
- **Calm.** Even when discussing serious health concerns, we don't catastrophize.
- **Specific.** Numbers, names, sources. "A 2023 study in *Environmental Health Perspectives* (n=412)..." not "research shows..."
- **Practical.** Every post answers "so what should I actually do?"
- **Slightly investigative.** We've done the digging. We can tell you what we found.
- **Confident but not arrogant.** We've made up our minds and we'll defend our recommendations, but we acknowledge complexity.

### How we don't sound
- ❌ Performatively scared ("I was HORRIFIED to learn...")
- ❌ Tribal ("Big Pharma doesn't want you to know")
- ❌ Vague ("there are some concerns about...")
- ❌ Wellness-influencer-coded ("✨ glow up your home ✨")
- ❌ Mom-blog-coded ("As a mama of two...")
- ❌ Conspiratorial
- ❌ Fluffy

### Side-by-side examples

**❌ Don't write this:**
> If you're like me, you probably had no idea that the cookware you've been using for years could be slowly poisoning your family. I was SHOCKED when I learned about the chemicals lurking in non-stick pans! 😱 Mama bear instincts kicked in and I knew I had to do something.

**✅ Write this:**
> Most non-stick cookware is coated with PFAS — a class of synthetic chemicals that includes PFOA, the compound DuPont paid $670 million to settle litigation over in 2017. The good news: replacing it is one of the easiest non-toxic kitchen swaps you can make. The better news: you don't have to spend $400 on a fancy ceramic set to do it well.

---

**❌ Don't write this:**
> Studies have shown that microplastics may be linked to a number of health issues. While more research is needed, many experts recommend reducing exposure when possible.

**✅ Write this:**
> A 2024 study in *Environment International* (n=257) found microplastics in human placental tissue at average concentrations of 12.6 particles per gram. The same research group documented microplastics in arterial plaques in 2023 and linked their presence to a 4.5x higher cardiovascular event rate over 34 months. The mechanism isn't fully understood. The exposure is documented.

## 5. Visual identity

### Colors
- **Primary**: Sage `#7A8B6F`
- **Accent**: Warm cream `#F4EFE6`
- **Deep**: Forest `#2C3E2F`
- **Highlight**: Terracotta `#C97D4F` (used sparingly, for callouts and CTAs)
- **Text**: Charcoal `#1F1F1F` on cream, never pure black

### Typography
- **Headlines**: A serif with personality. Recommend **Fraunces** or **Tiempos Headline**. Not Playfair (too overused).
- **Body**: A clean humanist sans. Recommend **Inter** or **Söhne**. Body copy at 18px minimum, line-height 1.7.
- **Captions and meta**: Same sans, smaller, slightly muted

### Photography style
- **Real, not stock.** Natural light, real homes, real hands, no models with perfect manicures
- **Materials over people.** Glass jars, linen, wood, ceramic, brass — the textures of a non-toxic kitchen
- **Slightly imperfect.** A worn cutting board. A spice jar with the label peeling. Lived-in, not performative
- **Soft natural light.** Morning or golden hour. Never harsh studio light, never flash
- **Earth tones.** No bright primary colors, no neon, no stark white backgrounds
- **No people-as-props.** If a person is in a photo, they're using something — not modeling it

### What our pages look like
- Generous whitespace
- Long-form content with a sticky table of contents on desktop
- Inline callout boxes for "key takeaway" sections (cream background, sage border)
- Citation footnotes that don't interrupt the reading flow
- Minimal ads (none in months 1-6)
- Clean, single-column reading experience

### What we don't look like
- Pinterest mommy blog (florals, script fonts, "hey friend" tone)
- Health website (stock photo doctor, white coat, stethoscope)
- Aggressive affiliate site (giant banner ads, "DEAL!" badges, popups)
- Wellness influencer (millennial pink, "manifestation," chakras)

## 6. Editorial principles (PFL-specific, on top of network principles)

1. **No fearmongering. Ever.** We can describe a real risk in a calm voice. We do not write "the SHOCKING TRUTH about..." headlines. We do not use alarm caps in the body.

2. **Two sources minimum for any health claim.** One peer-reviewed study, one regulatory body (EPA, FDA, EWG, EU REACH). No exceptions.

3. **Acknowledge uncertainty.** When the science is mixed, we say so. "The evidence on X is mixed: studies A and B suggest [finding], while study C found [opposite]. Here's what we recommend in the meantime."

4. **Always rank.** Every comparison post has a clear #1 pick, alternates, and a budget option. We commit.

5. **Update quarterly.** Every comparison page gets a quarterly review. Products change formulations. New brands enter. We update or we lose authority.

6. **Disclose tested vs researched.** If we physically tested a product, we say so. If we evaluated it from research, we say so. Never blur the line.

7. **Steel-man the opposition.** When recommending against a product or category, we acknowledge the strongest counter-argument and address it.

## 7. Headlines and titles

### Title formulas that work for PlasticFreeLab

- *The X best [product] of [year], tested and ranked*
- *We tested X [products]. These [smaller number] actually [meet criteria].*
- *[Product] vs [product]: which is actually [criterion]?*
- *The truth about [controversial topic]: [calm answer]*
- *[Number] [thing] you can stop worrying about (and [number] you can't)*
- *What's actually in your [product]? A [year] investigation*

### Headlines we don't use

- ❌ "You won't BELIEVE what's hiding in your kitchen!"
- ❌ "The TOXIC truth about [product]"
- ❌ "Why I'll never buy [product] again"
- ❌ "[Product] is KILLING you"
- ❌ "Doctors HATE this one weird trick"
- ❌ Anything in all caps
- ❌ Anything with more than one exclamation point in the entire post

## 9. Reader journey

1. **Reader finds us via Pinterest or Google search** — looking for a specific answer ("best non-toxic cookware," "is fluoride toothpaste safe")
2. **Reader lands on a comparison page or pillar** — sees the calm tone, the citations, the clear recommendation
3. **Reader reads to the recommendation** — leaves with an answer, possibly clicks an affiliate link if it's a product they were going to buy anyway
4. **Reader sees email capture** — "Get our quarterly non-toxic kitchen audit checklist" or similar genuinely useful lead magnet
5. **Reader subscribes** — receives one email a week with a single useful thing
6. **Reader returns** — for the next question
7. **Long-term: reader becomes a buyer** of any house product we eventually launch (water filter, glass storage, etc.)

The lead magnet for Wave 1: **"The PlasticFreeLab Kitchen Swap Audit"** — a printable PDF checklist of every kitchen item to evaluate, with a clear "swap now / swap eventually / don't bother" tier for each.

## 10. Pinterest strategy

Pinterest is the primary distribution channel for PlasticFreeLab. The visual identity above translates directly to pin design.

### Pin formats
- **Carousel pins**: 5-image swipes for "swap audits" and listicles
- **Standard pins**: 1000×1500, headline overlay on a real photograph
- **Idea pins**: 9-frame stories for the more ambitious posts

### Pin headline formulas
- *The [number] [items] I threw out of my kitchen this year (and what I replaced them with)*
- *[Number] non-toxic [category] that actually work (tested in [year])*
- *Save this: the [category] buying guide nobody else will give you*
- *Before you buy [product], read this*

### Pin pinning cadence
- 3 pin variants per published post
- 5-10 pins/day to the main board
- Tailwind or native Pinterest scheduler
- Group boards as a secondary push (find 10 active non-toxic living group boards in week 2)
